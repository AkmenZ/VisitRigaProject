Things to See

Most of the major sites are located in the Old Town, which is a UNESCO protected area with unique buildings and architectural masterpieces. This part of town has many cafés and restaurants so it’s easy to spend a whole day exploring without having to venture outside for lunch or dinner. Aside from sightseeing, there’s a great selection of spas and wellness, historic buildings and markets. You will never run out of things to do! Whether you have a week or only a few days, here’s a list of the top things you should do when you’re in Riga. more information...

National Library

The NLL is a cultural, scientific and educational center of national importance, which safeguards the published heritage of Latvia and the world, ensures its public accessibility and promotes its creative use. The NLL provides to its clients user-oriented services in both physical and digital environment. To implement this vision the NLL co-operates with other libraries and cultural institutions of Latvia and engages in international cultural processes. more information...

Freedom Monument

The monument was funded entirely by donations from residents and constructed as a memorial to those who fell in Latvia’s struggle for independence. It was unveiled on November 18, 1935 as part of sculptor Karlis Zale’s project "Mirdzi kā zvaigzne!" (Shine Like a Star). The Freedom Monument symbolizes the idea of independence. The 42.7 meter high monument is made up of 56 sculptures, divided into 13 sculptural groups on four levels, which depict Latvian history and culture. more information...

The House of Blackheads

House of the Black Heads, a venue for exhibitions, concerts and other events, is a landmark of Riga Old City and the Latvian capital’s most magnificent edifice. It was built in 1334 as a venue for meetings and banquets held by Riga’s various public organizations. The building was destroyed by bombs in the Second World War but was fully rebuilt in 1999. more information...
